Life in SP

In SP's 38-hectare campus, there are a wide range of facilities that make your learning in SP conducive. You are definitely spoilt for food choice by the 6 food courts that you can find around campus, each with their own specialties!

Poly life is no doubt very different from secondary school. In SP's vibrant school population, you ought to meet and make many new friends.

Unlike secondary school, tutorials and lectures are spread out throughout the day and not confined from morning to afternoon (See my timetable below!). Instead of taking your regular STEM subjects, you will be doing a different set of modules every semester.

The SoC Experience


In School of Computing (SoC), you are able to learn different areas of IT such as app development, artificial intelligence and cyber security. You will also be equipped with computational thinking and programming skills that will be useful when you move into the IT industry in the future.


In my course (DISM), I learnt about networks, JavaScript, web development and computing mathematics in my first semester. In the following semester, I will be covering course-specific modules such as cryptography and computer law and investigation.


As a DISM student, it also means that most of our time is spent in the state-of-the-art Cyber Wargame Center!


I joined the SOC Ambassadors who are in charge of organising school events and outreach programmes to secondary schools. There, I also made friends with people from other courses.
